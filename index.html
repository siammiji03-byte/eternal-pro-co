The 5 leaks

Why most Airbnb hosts leave money on the table

If two or more of these sound familiar, you have a systems problem — not a hosting problem.

01 — PRICING

Your pricing isn't adapting to demand

Static or seasonally-set rates miss the daily shifts in demand, weekends, events, and shoulder seasons. The result is consistent revenue loss every month.

02 — MESSAGING

Guest communication is largely manual

You answer the same questions every week. Slow replies push your response score down and your conversion rate with it.

03 — OPERATIONS

Cleaning runs on follow-ups, not systems

Turnovers depend on WhatsApp chasing instead of structure. One missed clean is one bad review that costs you a month of bookings.

04 — REVIEWS

Inconsistent post-booking communication

Reviews come when they come. Without a structured post-stay flow, you average 4.7 instead of 4.9 — and lose Superhost lift.

05 — DEPENDENCY

Heavy reliance on Airbnb

Rising fees, limited control, and zero ownership of your repeat guests. Without a direct booking channel, you're permanently exposed to platform changes.

Running an Airbnb shouldn't feel like a 24/7 job.
